#### Plan Differences
Both plans contain 10 logical joins. Broadcast joins map 1:1 (Comet 7 BroadcastHashJoin vs Gluten 7 BroadcastHashJoinExecTransformer; both have 1 broadcast SubqueryBroadcast for DPP). The structural difference is the strategy chosen for the 3 non-broadcast joins:

- Comet: 2 `SortMergeJoin` + 1 `CometSortMergeJoin` (with 5 `CometSort` + 7 `CometExchange`).
- Gluten: 3 `ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer` (with 8 `ColumnarExchange`, 8 `VeloxResizeBatches`, 0 sort operators).

Pipeline shape: Comet has 13 `CometNativeColumnarToRow` boundaries; Gluten has only 3 `VeloxColumnarToRow` and runs a fully columnar pipeline through 18 `WholeStageCodegenTransformer` stages plus 15 `InputIteratorTransformer` boundaries.

Scan/filter ops also differ in counts: Comet has 13 `CometFilter` + 7 `CometProject`; Gluten has 13 `FilterExecTransformer` + 20 `ProjectExecTransformer` + 9 fewer plain `Project` (Gluten splits projection into more nodes). Aggregation: Comet 6 `CometHashAggregate` vs Gluten 3 `FlushableHashAggregateExecTransformer` + 3 `RegularHashAggregateExecTransformer`.

Node counts: Comet 102 / 100 edges; Gluten 130 / 114 edges (+28 nodes / +14 edges).

Input volume: Gluten reads 17.6 GB to produce the same record counts Comet produces from 12.1 GB. Most of that extra is in the catalog_sales scan stage (Comet 657.9 MB → Gluten 3.6 GB for 432,006,840 records) and the inventory scan stage (Comet 2.6 GB → Gluten 5.1 GB for 1,033,560,000 records).

#### Root Cause
The query's runtime is dominated by a single 200-task stage that pipelines the 1B-record join chain into the final pre-aggregation shuffle. The join-strategy choice for the three non-broadcast joins differs between the two apps, and that choice — combined with Velox's per-record processing rate inside that pipeline — is what makes Comet 5.95× faster on this query:

1. Comet plans the three non-broadcast joins as **SortMergeJoin** (2 plain `SortMergeJoin` + 1 `CometSortMergeJoin`, fed by 5 `CometSort` and 7 `CometExchange` operators). The pipeline streams sorted input through the joins; the corresponding stage 9922 finishes 200 uniform tasks in 7.6s p50.
2. Gluten plans the same three joins as **ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer** (no sort operators in the entire plan). Stage 9881 must consume the full shuffle stream into hash tables and then probe; the 200 uniform tasks take 1m25s p50 — 11.2x slower per task at ~59K records/s/task vs Comet's ~666K records/s/task.
3. There is no skew (Gluten p75/p25 = 1.06x, max/p50 = 1.11x), no spill (0 B disk, 0 B memory in both apps), no GC pressure (Gluten 0 ms in this stage), and no resource contention signal (Peak Exec Memory 248–264 MB, well below the 6.87 GB task off-heap budget). Tasks are uniformly slow — a per-record processing-rate problem inside the columnar pipeline, not a distribution/resource problem.
4. Secondary contributor: Gluten reads +5.5 GB more raw Parquet input than Comet for identical record counts (catalog_sales 657.9 MB → 3.6 GB at 432M records; inventory 2.6 GB → 5.1 GB at 1.03B records). This adds ~3s of upstream scan cost but is dwarfed by item 2.

Comet's 5.95× lead is concentrated in stage 9881 where Gluten/Velox executes the three non-broadcast joins as ShuffledHashJoin within a fully columnar Velox pipeline at ~59K records/s/task, while Comet executes the equivalent stage 9922 as SortMergeJoin at ~666K records/s/task. That single per-task slowdown accounts for ~98% of the SQL-level delta.
