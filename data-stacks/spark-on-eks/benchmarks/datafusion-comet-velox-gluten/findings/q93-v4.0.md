#### Plan Differences
Both plans run identical logical work: scan store_sales (8.64B rows) and store_returns (12.4M rows after filter), shuffle on `(item_sk, ticket_number)`, join, broadcast-join with the filtered `reason` table (1 row), partial+final aggregation on `ss_customer_sk`, take-ordered-100. Three Bloom-filter / runtime-filter sub-queries are equivalent.

The decisive structural difference is the join algorithm for the big `store_sales ⋈ store_returns` join:

| Step (after equivalent shuffles)                           | Comet                              | Gluten Velox                          |
|------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Sort store_sales side (8.64B rows / ~293 GB stage stats)   | CometSort (5)                      | — (none)                              |
| Sort store_returns side (12.4M rows)                       | CometSort (11)                     | — (none)                              |
| Equi-join of the two shuffled inputs                       | CometSortMergeJoin (12)            | ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer (18)  |

Operator-count diff confirms: `CometSortMergeJoin -1`, `CometSort -2` on the A side; `ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer +1`, no sort transformer on the B side. All other operator differences are 1:1 columnar-engine substitutions (CometExchange↔ColumnarExchange+VeloxResizeBatches, CometHashAggregate↔Flushable/RegularHashAggregateExecTransformer, CometBroadcastHashJoin↔BroadcastHashJoinExecTransformer, CometNativeColumnarToRow↔VeloxColumnarToRow, CometTakeOrderedAndProject↔TakeOrderedAndProjectExecTransformer). The +6 stages / +7811 tasks in Gluten come from Gluten emitting extra stage boundaries around its hash-shuffle sub-stages and skipped reuse stages (3 of 14 Gluten stages were AQE-skipped, e.g. 10412, 10415, 10417).

#### Root Cause

The 70% / 3.3× slowdown is concentrated in the single shuffled join stage (Comet 2704 = 121s vs Gluten 10414 = 13s; that 108-second gap is essentially the entire 108-second query-duration delta of 156s vs 47s).

In that stage Comet picks a **Sort-Merge Join** (`CometSortMergeJoin` with two preceding `CometSort` nodes) on the 8.64B-row store_sales side joined with the 12.4M-row store_returns side, while Gluten Velox picks a **Shuffled Hash Join** (`ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer`, no sort step). Both engines receive the same hash-partitioned shuffle inputs (200 partitions, identical row counts on the shuffle-write side: Comet 156.7 GB / Gluten 162.2 GB writes upstream).

The everything-equal upstream stages confirm the engines are otherwise comparable on this workload: scan + columnar shuffle-write of store_sales costs Comet 29.15s and Gluten 30.37s (Comet is ~4% faster); store_returns scan+filter is 6.7s vs 5.0s. Per-task input throughput in the scan stage is essentially identical (p50 task 955ms vs 1053ms; p50 input 24.7 MB vs 24.7 MB).

Per-task in stage 2704, every quantile from p25 to max is ~51–57 seconds slower in Comet (5.3s → 56s at p25; 10.5s → 67s at max) with no spill, no GC, and no skew on either side. The cost is therefore the per-row work of the SMJ algorithm itself: Comet must fully sort both shuffled inputs (the larger being 293 GiB / 8.64B rows distributed across 200 partitions ≈ ~1.5 GiB / 43M rows per task to sort) before producing any merge output, whereas Gluten builds an in-memory hash table on the smaller side (~240 MB total ≈ ~1.2 MB/partition, matching the 80 MB peak-exec-memory reading on Gluten's join task) and streams the larger side through it. The plan-level diff (`CometSortMergeJoin -1`, `CometSort -2` vs `ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer +1`) is the only structural divergence in the join pipeline, and it accounts for the entire stage-time and query-time difference.
