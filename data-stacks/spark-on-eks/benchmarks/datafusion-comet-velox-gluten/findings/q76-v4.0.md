#### Plan Differences
Plan size grew from 40 nodes / 43 edges (Comet) to 73 nodes / 66 edges (Gluten Velox), +33 nodes / +23 edges.

Operator-level mapping for the 5-table union (catalog_sales, store_sales, web_sales joined to date_dim and item):

- Scans: Comet's 5 `CometNativeScan parquet` are replaced by 5 `FileSourceScanExecTransformer parquet` in Gluten (1 per fact/dim table).
- Filter/Project: Comet's 5 `CometFilter` + 9 `CometProject` become 5 `FilterExecTransformer` + 13 `ProjectExecTransformer`.
- Broadcast joins: 6 `CometBroadcastHashJoin` -> 6 `BroadcastHashJoinExecTransformer`; 2 `CometBroadcastExchange` -> 2 `ColumnarBroadcastExchange`.
- Aggregation: 2 `CometHashAggregate` (single op) are replaced with a split partial/final pair: 1 `FlushableHashAggregateExecTransformer` + 1 `RegularHashAggregateExecTransformer`.
- Shuffle: 5 `CometExchange` -> 5 `ColumnarExchange` + 5 `AQEShuffleRead` (+3 vs Comet's 2) + 5 `VeloxResizeBatches`.
- Top-level: 1 `CometUnion` -> 1 `ColumnarUnion`; 1 `CometTakeOrderedAndProject` -> 1 `TakeOrderedAndProjectExecTransformer`; 1 `CometNativeColumnarToRow` -> 1 `VeloxColumnarToRow`.
- Stage wrapping: Gluten adds 10 `WholeStageCodegenTransformer` and 11 `InputIteratorTransformer` (Comet has 0 of each).

Aggregate per-task duration for the three large scan+partial-agg stages is consistently 31-53% higher in Gluten despite Comet processing more rows of shuffle output (Comet shuffle write is 2.4 GB vs Gluten 1.6 GB; per-task input is comparable, e.g. p50 input 24.4 MB vs 24.6 MB on the 3193-task stage). Stage time totals: Comet 1m41.725s vs Gluten 2m37.545s (+55.82s).

The post-shuffle final-aggregation stage shows the largest single-stage divergence: Comet runs it across 600 AQE-coalesced tasks in 1.202s (p50 206ms, max 480ms). Gluten runs it across only 157 tasks in 14.597s reading 1.6 GB of shuffle, with task-quantile distribution p25/p50/p75/max = 660ms / 747ms / 771ms / 13.906s — a single straggler accounts for ~13s, while Comet has no equivalent outlier.

Total shuffle read is 1.6 GB in Gluten (matches the 1.6 GB combined shuffle write of stages 7435/7437/7438) versus 58 MB in Comet. Comet's 2.4 GB of shuffle write is consumed by stage 7471 without registering in the shuffle-read counter (it shows 0 B input/0 B shuffle read in stage 7471 yet writes 54.3 MB onward to stage 7476), implying Comet routes the union+final-aggregate via local pipelining rather than a full shuffle read. Disk spill is 0 in both engines.

GC Time is 391ms in Comet vs 25ms in Gluten, so JVM-side GC is not implicated.

#### Root Cause
The +26.663s gap (Comet 1.69× faster) decomposes into two compounding factors, both visible in stage-level metrics.

1. Per-task slowdown across every scan + partial-aggregate + shuffle-write stage. With near-identical input volumes (118.6 GB vs 124.8 GB) and identical task counts (3193 / 1380 / 2898), Gluten's p50 task duration is 31% / 39% / 53% higher (413→541ms, 521→725ms, 496→758ms). This adds +29.6s of stage time across the three stages. The plan diff shows Gluten replaces each `CometHashAggregate` with a `FlushableHashAggregateExecTransformer` -> `ColumnarExchange` -> `VeloxResizeBatches` -> `AQEShuffleRead` -> `RegularHashAggregateExecTransformer` chain wrapped in `WholeStageCodegenTransformer`/`InputIteratorTransformer` boundaries (+33 plan nodes overall), while Comet keeps a single fused `CometHashAggregate` -> `CometExchange`. The extra columnar transitions and resize-batches operators are the structural source of the per-task slowdown and account for the +55.82s total stage-time delta.

2. Final-aggregation stage straggler. Comet's post-shuffle aggregation runs across 600 AQE-coalesced tasks in 1.202s with no shuffle-read tracked (pipelined). Gluten's equivalent stage 7444 coalesces to only 157 tasks, reads the full 1.6 GB of shuffle, and the task distribution is sharply skewed (p75 771ms vs max 13.906s); the lone straggler runs 18x the p75 and dominates the 14.597s stage runtime. This contributes +13.4s on its own.

Combined, the per-task slowdown in the three big partial-aggregation stages (+~30s of stage time, attributable to the heavier columnar pipeline) and the skewed 14.6s final-aggregation stage with under-partitioned AQE coalescing produce the observed 33.452s -> 60.115s wall-clock gap, with Comet 1.69× faster overall. Shuffle volume is actually smaller in Gluten (1.6 GB vs 2.4 GB written) and spill/GC are not factors.
