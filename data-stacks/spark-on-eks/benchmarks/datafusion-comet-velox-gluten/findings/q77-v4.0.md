#### Root Cause
Same 14 stages, same task counts (~622 vs 611), same 2.8 GB input, and ~3x lower shuffle volume on Gluten — yet Gluten runs 1.186s slower (2.002s → 3.188s, Comet 1.61× faster). Comet's lead is concentrated in the six `genShuffleDependency` scan/aggregate feeder stages that read store_sales (1.5 GB/184M rows), catalog_sales (766 MB/96M), and web_sales (432 MB/48M). On the largest of these (stage 7454 vs Comet 7481), Gluten's per-task durations are ~2x Comet's at every quantile (p50 921ms vs 443ms; max 1.418s vs 0.692s) on byte-identical input, and the wall-clock gap on this single stage (+0.730s) alone explains most of the 1.186s query gap.

The plan diff explains why per-task work is more expensive on Gluten: each scan path is wrapped in additional operators that don't exist in the Comet plan — `WholeStageCodegenTransformer` + `FileSourceScanExecTransformer` + `FilterExecTransformer` + extra `ProjectExecTransformer`s (28 vs 13), plus a split aggregate (`FlushableHashAggregateExecTransformer` + `RegularHashAggregateExecTransformer` instead of a single `CometHashAggregate`), plus 7 `VeloxResizeBatches` and 21 `InputIteratorTransformer` wrappers, and a `VeloxColumnarToRow` at the boundary. The plan grows from 104 to 154 nodes (+48%) for the same query. With ~6.2 MB of total shuffle and 0 spill, this is a CPU-bound scan/agg pipeline, and the additional per-batch operator overhead in Gluten's WSCG transformer chain — not data movement — produces the 2x per-task slowdown that puts Comet 1.61× ahead.
