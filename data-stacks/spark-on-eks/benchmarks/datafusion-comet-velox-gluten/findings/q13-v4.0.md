#### Plan Differences
Plan size: A=66 nodes / 64 edges, B=85 nodes / 75 edges (+19 nodes, +11 edges).

Operator replacements:
- Scans (6): all 6 `CometNativeScan` → `FileSourceScanExecTransformer` (store_sales, store, date_dim, customer_address, customer_demographics×2, household_demographics×2). One scan also gets a `ColumnarSubqueryBroadcast` (+1) on the Velox side.
- Filters / Projects: 8 `CometFilter` + 5 `CometProject` → 8 `FilterExecTransformer` + 10 `ProjectExecTransformer`.
- Aggregates: 4 `CometHashAggregate` + 2 Spark `HashAggregate` → 3 `FlushableHashAggregateExecTransformer` + 3 `RegularHashAggregateExecTransformer` (Gluten splits each aggregate into a flushable partial + a regular final).
- Joins (4 total):
  - 3 `BroadcastHashJoin` (Spark JVM fallback in Comet) + 1 `CometBroadcastHashJoin` → 4 `BroadcastHashJoinExecTransformer` (all native in Gluten).
  - 1 `SortMergeJoin` (Comet) → 1 `ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer` (Gluten changes the algorithm: SMJ → SHJ; both `CometSort` operators that fed the SMJ are gone in B).
- Exchanges: 4 `CometExchange` + 1 `Exchange` (5 total) → 5 `ColumnarExchange`; 3 `BroadcastExchange` + 1 `CometBroadcastExchange` → 4 `ColumnarBroadcastExchange`.
- Conversions / batch plumbing: 7 `CometNativeColumnarToRow` → 3 `VeloxColumnarToRow`; B adds 5 `VeloxResizeBatches` and 9 `InputIteratorTransformer` (no Comet equivalents).
- Codegen: A has Spark `WholeStageCodegen (1)`+`(2)`; B has 12 `WholeStageCodegenTransformer (1..12)` wrappers around the Velox pipelines.

Net effect on shape: +1 SMJ→SHJ flip + flushable/final aggregate split + extra `VeloxResizeBatches`/`InputIteratorTransformer` plumbing produces +7 stages and +1447 tasks (817 → 2264) for the same query.

#### Root Cause
Comet's lead is dominated by the per-task cost of the Velox native scan/filter/partial-aggregate over `store_sales`: at the same 1.64 B input rows and near-identical per-task input size (50.2 MB vs 50.7 MB at p50), Gluten/Velox runs 1.458 s/task vs Comet's 894 ms/task — a ~+63 % per-task slowdown — even with 152–160 MB peak execution memory (vs Comet's reported 0 B). Multiplied across 635 tasks on 115 cores this single stage costs +3.383 s, ~78 % of the total +4.340 s gap.

The remaining ~0.8 s comes from a structural plan change: Gluten converts the `store_sales × customer_demographics` join from `SortMergeJoin` (Comet, 1 op, with `CometSort`s pre-sorted in the dominant stage) to `ShuffledHashJoinExecTransformer` (Gluten), forcing an extra shuffle cluster (stages 8246/8247/8248, 84.1 MB shuffle read, 1.949 s) where Comet's post-shuffle finish takes only 1.133 s. The same plan rewrite plus the flushable/final aggregate split, the addition of `VeloxResizeBatches` (5) and `InputIteratorTransformer` (9), and the SHJ shuffle drive the +7 stages and +1447 tasks (817 → 2264) and the +84.3 MB shuffle-read seen at the SQL level — though those shuffle volumes are too small (84.6 MB total) to be the primary cost; the cost is the per-task scan/agg slowdown in stage 8245.
